How to get help if you suspect having COVID-19

print

About consultation desk for COVID-19

There are three types of consultations based on symptoms and circumstances.

  • You are experiencing symptoms and have a primary care doctor
  • You are experiencing symptoms and do not have a primary care doctor
  • Those who feel uneasy.

If you have a primary care doctor and have the following:

  • Have symptoms of a relatively mild cold such as a fever or cough If you are pregnant or have an underlying disease such as diabetes, heart failure, respiratory disease (COPD, etc.), dialysis, immunosuppressive drugs, or anti-cancer drugs, please consult with us immediately. Otherwise, if your symptoms last more than 4 days, consult with us.
  • There is strong symptoms such as shortness of breath (dyspnea), strong lethargy, high fever, etc. Symptoms vary from person to person, so if you think it's a strong symptom, you should consult with them immediately. The same is true for those who have to continue to take antipyretics.

Please call the medical institution where you receive your daily medical care. (Even if you don't have any of the above symptoms, if you have concerns, please call your regular medical institution for advice.

If you do not have a primary care doctor and have the following:

  • Have symptoms of a relatively mild cold such as a fever or cough If you are pregnant or have an underlying disease such as diabetes, heart failure, respiratory disease (COPD, etc.), dialysis, immunosuppressive drugs, or anti-cancer drugs, please consult with us immediately. Otherwise, if your symptoms last more than 4 days, consult with us.
  • There is strong symptoms such as shortness of breath (dyspnea), strong lethargy, high fever, etc. Symptoms vary from person to person, so if you think it's a strong symptom, you should consult with them immediately. The same is true for those who have to continue to take antipyretics.

If you are asked by Ibaraki COVID-19 Consultation Hotline to visit COVID-19 Outpatient (Traveller/Contact Outpatient) Services or PCR Testing Center

A doctor will determine the need for testing. Wear a mask and avoid using public transportation as much as possible to get to the hospital.

After a doctor determines that a test is needed, you receive a PCR test at the Tokyo Metropolitan Institute of Public Health or elsewhere, and you:

Test positive

The patient will be admitted to a designated medical institution or will receive medical treatment in an accommodation facility.

Test negative

Stay at home and see a doctor if necessary.

However, if the symptoms do not get better, please consult the consultation desk or your doctor again.
